Detailed 3-Day Beginner Program

A 3-day beginner chess course for students who are new to chess.

Class Length 1 hour each day
Materials Computer, wall chess board, chairs, students
Course Goal Students learn piece movement, rules, common ideas, and how to think before moves.

Day 1: Meet the Pieces

Students learn the goal of chess, the chessboard layout, how every piece moves, and basic vocabulary.

0:00-0:05

Welcome

  • Introduce AK64 Chess Hub.
  • Ask who has seen chess before and who knows any piece names.
  • Explain that chess is a strategy game and the goal is checkmate.
0:05-0:15

Understanding the Board

  • Teach 8 rows, 8 columns, light square on the right, files, ranks, and diagonals.
  • Ask students to identify corners, center squares, and diagonals on the wall board.
0:15-0:35

Learning the Pieces

  • Pawn: moves forward and captures diagonally.
  • Rook: moves horizontally and vertically.
  • Bishop: moves diagonally.
  • Knight: moves in an L shape and can jump.
  • Queen: combines rook and bishop movement.
  • King: moves one square.
  • After each piece, students demonstrate legal moves on the wall board.
0:35-0:50

Piece Challenges

  • Ask how many squares a rook can move to.
  • Ask where a knight can move.
  • Ask whether a bishop can reach a target square.
0:50-1:00

Review

  • Review piece names, movement, and the goal of chess.
  • Quiz: Which piece jumps? Which piece is worth the most? Which piece must never be lost?

Day 2: Rules and Chess Principles

Students learn check, checkmate, stalemate, special rules, and AK64's basic chess principles.

0:00-0:10

Review

  • Review all pieces with wall-board questions.
  • Ask how bishops and knights move.
0:10-0:25

Check and Checkmate

  • Explain check, checkmate, and stalemate using simple positions.
  • Ask whether the king is in check, whether it can escape, and whether the position is checkmate.
0:25-0:35

Special Rules

  • Castling: why we castle and when it is allowed.
  • Promotion: what happens when a pawn reaches the end.
  • En passant: brief introduction.
0:35-0:55

AK64's Basic Chess Principles

  • Golden Rule 1: Control the center.
  • Golden Rule 2: Develop your pieces.
  • Golden Rule 3: Castle early.
  • Golden Rule 4: Protect your pieces.
  • Golden Rule 5: Think before you move.
  • Checklist: Can I give check? Can I capture something? Is one of my pieces attacked? What is my opponent threatening? Is my king safe?
0:55-1:00

Review

  • Ask students to name the five golden rules.
  • Review checkmate and why castling matters.

Day 3: Beginner Tactics and Chess Thinking

Students learn how to spot opportunities, avoid mistakes, and recognize basic tactical patterns.

0:00-0:10

Review

  • Review piece movement, check, checkmate, and chess principles.
0:10-0:25

Forks

  • Explain that a fork is when one piece attacks two pieces at once.
  • Show knight and queen examples.
  • Challenge students to find the fork.
0:25-0:35

Pins

  • Explain that a piece is pinned when it cannot move because something important is behind it.
  • Show examples and ask which piece is pinned.
0:35-0:45

Skewers

  • Explain that a skewer happens when a valuable piece moves away and another piece behind it can be captured.
  • Show examples on the wall board.
0:45-0:55

Spot the Mistake

  • Create positions on the wall board.
  • Ask which piece is hanging, what wins material, and what threat exists.
0:55-1:00

Graduation Review

  • Review the goal of chess, knight movement, checkmate, forks, and AK64's five golden rules.
  • Congratulate students on completing the AK64 Beginner Course.

Key Teaching Philosophy

Keep students involved constantly. Avoid speaking for more than 3-5 minutes without asking a question or inviting a student to the wall board. The more students interact with the board, the faster they learn.
