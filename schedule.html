Detailed Beginner Lesson Plan

A planned beginner chess lesson path for students who are new to chess.

Class Length 1 hour each class
Materials Computer, wall chess board, chairs, students
Course Goal Students will learn piece movement, rules, common ideas, and how to think before moves.

Rules and Chess Principles

Students will learn check, checkmate, stalemate, special rules, and AK64's basic chess principles.

0:00-0:10

Review

  • Review all pieces with wall-board questions.
  • Ask how bishops and knights move.
0:10-0:25

Check and Checkmate

  • Explain check, checkmate, and stalemate using simple positions.
  • Ask whether the king is in check, whether it can escape, and whether the position is checkmate.
0:25-0:35

Special Rules

  • Castling: why we castle and when it is allowed.
  • Promotion: what happens when a pawn reaches the end.
  • En passant: brief introduction.
0:35-0:55

AK64's Basic Chess Principles

  • Golden Rule 1: Control the center.
  • Golden Rule 2: Develop your pieces.
  • Golden Rule 3: Castle early.
  • Golden Rule 4: Protect your pieces.
  • Golden Rule 5: Think before you move.
  • Checklist: Can I give check? Can I capture something? Is one of my pieces attacked? What is my opponent threatening? Is my king safe?
0:55-1:00

Review

  • Ask students to name the five golden rules.
  • Review checkmate and why castling matters.

Beginner Tactics and Chess Thinking

Students will learn how to spot opportunities, avoid mistakes, and recognize basic tactical patterns.

0:00-0:10

Review

  • Review piece movement, check, checkmate, and chess principles.
0:10-0:25

Forks

  • Explain that a fork is when one piece attacks two pieces at once.
  • Show knight and queen examples.
  • Challenge students to find the fork.
0:25-0:35

Pins

  • Explain that a piece is pinned when it cannot move because something important is behind it.
  • Show examples and ask which piece is pinned.
0:35-0:45

Skewers

  • Explain that a skewer happens when a valuable piece moves away and another piece behind it can be captured.
  • Show examples on the wall board.
0:45-0:55

Spot the Mistake

  • Create positions on the wall board.
  • Ask which piece is hanging, what wins material, and what threat exists.
0:55-1:00

Lesson Review

  • Review the goal of chess, knight movement, checkmate, forks, and AK64's five golden rules.
  • Preview how future classes can build on the beginner lesson path.

Key Teaching Philosophy

Keep students involved constantly. Avoid speaking for more than 3-5 minutes without asking a question or inviting a student to the wall board. The more students interact with the board, the faster they learn.
